**📌 Project Overview**

Electricity markets play a crucial role in balancing energy supply and demand while ensuring economic efficiency and sustainability. This repository contains a comprehensive technical report and presentation analyzing the structure, mechanisms, challenges, and future trends of electricity markets, with a specific focus on India's power sector.This project was completed as part of the EE46104 Seminar and Technical Writing course during the 4th semester at the National Institute of Technology, Patna (NIT Patna).

**🔑 Key Topics Covered**

1. Market Structure & ParticipantsMarket Models: Regulated, Deregulated, and Hybrid markets.Key Stakeholders: Generators (e.g., NTPC, Tata Power), Transmission System Operators (TSOs like PGCIL), Distribution System Operators (DSOs/DISCOMs), Retailers, and Consumers.Market Segments: Wholesale (bulk trading) and Retail markets.
2. Pricing Mechanisms & TradingPricing Models: Marginal Pricing, Pay-as-Bid Pricing, and Capacity Markets.Trading Platforms: Day-Ahead Market (DAM) and Real-Time Market (RTM) facilitated by exchanges like IEX and PXIL.Contracts: Long-term PPAs, Medium-term, and Short-term bilateral contracts.
3. Current ChallengesRenewable Intermittency: Handling the variability of solar and wind energy and the lack of robust storage capacity.Grid Stability: Managing real-time balancing, transmission congestion, and unpredictable load patterns.Financial Health: The financial stress on DISCOMs due to high AT&C losses and subsidy dependence.
4. Innovations & The Future OutlookSmart Grids & Digitalization: Deployment of smart meters, IoT, and AI-driven advanced grid management.Energy Storage & EVs: The rise of Battery Energy Storage Systems (BESS) and the integration of Electric Vehicles (EVs) into the grid (V2G).Decentralization: The shift toward prosumers, rooftop solar adoption, and Peer-to-Peer (P2P) energy trading using blockchain.Decarbonization: India's push toward 500 GW of non-fossil fuel capacity by 2030 and overall net-zero goals.
